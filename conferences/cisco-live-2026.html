The Walkability Problem (and How to Solve It)

Mandalay Bay sits at the south end of the Las Vegas Strip. That matters because most Strip hotels are 1-3 miles north. A hotel that looks "close" on Google Maps might be a 30-minute walk through casino floors, across pedestrian bridges, and through parking garages. After 10 hours of sessions, that walk gets old fast.

The good news: a free tram connects Mandalay Bay to Luxor and Excalibur. From Excalibur, you can walk to MGM Grand and catch the Las Vegas Monorail north. Cisco also runs free shuttles to official conference hotels.

Pro tip: The free Mandalay Bay tram runs every 3-5 minutes and connects Mandalay Bay → Luxor → Excalibur. This makes Luxor and Excalibur effectively "connected" to the venue. It's enclosed and air-conditioned — critical in June Las Vegas heat.

Getting Around During Cisco Live

Free Mandalay Bay Tram

Connects Mandalay Bay → Luxor → Excalibur. Runs every 3-5 minutes. Air-conditioned. This is your best friend if you're staying at Luxor or Excalibur. No ticket needed — just walk on.

Las Vegas Monorail

Runs along the east side of the Strip from MGM Grand to the Sahara. Single ride is $5, day pass is $13. The nearest stop to Mandalay Bay is MGM Grand station — from there, walk to Excalibur and take the free tram. Total transit: ~15-20 min from mid-Strip hotels.

Cisco Shuttles

Cisco provides complimentary shuttle buses between official conference hotels and Mandalay Bay Convention Center throughout the conference week. Shuttle schedules are typically released in April/May. Check the Cisco Live Attend page for the latest schedule.

Rideshare (Uber/Lyft)

Expect $8-15 per ride to/from mid-Strip hotels. Surge pricing common during morning arrivals (8-9 AM) and evening departures (6-7 PM). Designated pickup areas are on the east side of Mandalay Bay. Budget $15-25/day if relying on rideshare.

June heat warning: Las Vegas averages 104°F in early June. Even a 10-minute outdoor walk will be uncomfortable in business attire. Stick to connected/tram hotels or budget for rideshare. Your shoes and your shirt will thank you.

When to Book (and How to Save)

Cisco Live draws 25,000-28,000 attendees to Las Vegas. Hotels near Mandalay Bay fill up fast, especially at discounted conference rates. Here's how to approach it:

Option 1: Cisco Official Hotel Block

Cisco negotiates discounted rates at partner hotels (Mandalay Bay, Luxor, MGM Grand, and others). Book through the Attendee Dashboard after registering. Pros: discounted rates, shuttle included. Cons: rates still include resort fees, limited room types.

Option 2: Book Independently (Often Cheaper)

Compare Cisco's block rates against Expedia, Hotels.com, or direct hotel sites. Outside the official block, you can sometimes find better rates — especially if you're flexible on exact hotel. This is also your best bet if the conference block is sold out.

Option 3: Off-Strip (Budget)

Hotels east of the Strip on Tropicana Ave or on Paradise Rd are significantly cheaper ($80-150/night vs $200-400 on the Strip). The tradeoff: you'll need a rideshare every day. If your company isn't covering the hotel, this can save $500+ over the conference week.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is Cisco Live 2026?

May 31 – June 4, 2026 at the Mandalay Bay Convention Center in Las Vegas, Nevada. Badge pickup opens the Saturday before (May 30).

How many people attend Cisco Live?

Cisco Live typically draws 25,000 – 28,000 attendees, making it one of the largest enterprise tech conferences in the world. Hotel availability near the venue gets tight — book early.

What's the best hotel for Cisco Live?

For convenience, Mandalay Bay can't be beat — you're inside the convention center. For budget, Luxor (one free tram stop away) offers the best value-to-proximity ratio. For quiet/working comfort, Delano's all-suite rooms are popular with repeat attendees.

Is Las Vegas hot in early June?

Yes. Average highs are 100-106°F. The convention center is fully air-conditioned, but walking between hotels and venues in business attire is uncomfortable. Prioritize connected hotels or plan for rideshare.

Do I need a car in Las Vegas for Cisco Live?

No. Between the free tram, Cisco shuttles, the monorail, and rideshare, a rental car is unnecessary and parking is expensive ($18-25/day). Save the money for a nicer hotel room.
